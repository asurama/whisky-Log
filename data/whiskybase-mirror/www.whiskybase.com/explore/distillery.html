Amrut Distilleries

India

Amrut crafts its whisky from Indian barley grown in Punjab and Rajasthan, nourished by Himalayan waters. Malted in Jaipur and Delhi, it is distilled in Bengaluru at 3,000 feet, accelerating maturation. Aged in bourbon casks and bottled without chill filtration, Amrut is known for its rich complexity and bold flavors.

Dornoch Distillery

Scotland

Founded by the Thompson brothers, Dornoch Distillery began in a converted fire station, reviving forgotten whisky-making techniques. Using organic heritage barley and an exceptionally long fermentation process, they craft whisky inspired by the pre-1960s era, focusing on depth and complexity.

Glenfarclas Distillery

Scotland

Glenfarclas Distillery is nestled in the heart of Speyside, Scotland, one of the longest-running independent distilleries in the country. Renowned for its rich, sherried single malts, Glenfarclas is one of the few distilleries that still use direct-fired stills, enhancing its bold and full-bodied character.

Daftmill Distillery

Scotland

Daftmill is a family-run farm distillery in the Scottish Lowlands, known for its small-scale, seasonal whisky production. The distillery uses barley grown on its own farm and water from a deep artesian well to craft high-quality, sought-after malt whiskies.

Glenkinchie Distillery

Scotland

Glenkinchie is a Lowland distillery, often referred as the “Edinburgh malt” due to its proximity to Scotland’s capital. Known for its light, floral, and grassy character, it produces whisky using one of Scotland’s largest pot stills, contributing to its delicate yet full-bodied profile.

New Riff Distilling

USA

New Riff Distillery is a modern distillery that embraces traditional bourbon and rye-making techniques with a bold, innovative spirit. Known for its bottled-in-bond whiskies, it adheres to strict production standards, ensuring full transparency and high quality.
